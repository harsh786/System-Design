## How to Use This Guide

This file maps directly to the phases in this repository. For each section, you'll find:
- **Free video courses** (YouTube, university lectures, MOOCs)
- **Paid courses** (Coursera, Udemy, Fast.ai)
- **Books** (classic and modern, free and paid)
- **Blogs and newsletters** (staying current)

**Time estimates** are approximate and assume part-time study (10-15 hrs/week).

## Phase 1: Mathematics Prerequisites

*Estimated time: 4-8 weeks*

### Video Courses (Free)

- **3Blue1Brown - Essence of Linear Algebra** (YouTube, 16 videos)
  - Best visual intuition for linear algebra ever created
  - Covers vectors, linear transformations, determinants, eigenvalues
  - https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDPD3MizzM2xVFitgF8hE_ab

- **3Blue1Brown - Essence of Calculus** (YouTube, 12 videos)
  - Visual calculus from first principles
  - Derivatives, integrals, Taylor series
  - https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDMsr9K-rj53DwVRMYO3t5Yr

- **Khan Academy - Statistics & Probability** (Free)
  - Comprehensive, starts from basics
  - Great for probability distributions, Bayes theorem, hypothesis testing
  - https://www.khanacademy.org/math/statistics-probability

- **MIT 18.06 Linear Algebra - Gilbert Strang** (MIT OCW)
  - The gold standard university course
  - Full lectures, problem sets, exams with solutions
  - https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-06-linear-algebra-spring-2010/

- **StatQuest with Josh Starmer** (YouTube)
  - Statistics and ML explained clearly with humor
  - Start with probability, then distributions, then ML topics
  - https://www.youtube.com/c/joshstarmer

- **MIT 18.01 Single Variable Calculus** (MIT OCW)
  - Full university calculus course
  - https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-01sc-single-variable-calculus-fall-2010/

- **Imperial College London - Mathematics for Machine Learning** (Coursera)
  - Three-course specialization: Linear Algebra, Multivariate Calculus, PCA
  - https://www.coursera.org/specializations/mathematics-machine-learning

### Books

- **"Mathematics for Machine Learning"** - Deisenroth, Faisal, Ong
  - FREE PDF: https://mml-book.github.io/
  - Covers exactly what you need for ML, nothing more
  - Best starting point for self-study

- **"Linear Algebra Done Right"** - Sheldon Axler
  - Proof-based, builds deep understanding
  - Good for those wanting rigorous foundations

- **"All of Statistics"** - Larry Wasserman
  - Concise statistics reference
  - Bridges stats and ML nicely

- **"Convex Optimization"** - Boyd & Vandenberghe
  - FREE: https://web.stanford.edu/~boyd/cvxbook/
  - Essential for understanding optimization in ML

- **"Calculus"** - James Stewart
  - Classic comprehensive calculus textbook
  - Good reference, not needed cover-to-cover

### Practice

- Khan Academy exercises (integrated with videos)
- MIT OCW problem sets (with solutions)
- 3Blue1Brown exercises on Grant's website

## Recommended Learning Order for Videos

If you only have time for a few courses, do these in order:

1. **3Blue1Brown** (Linear Algebra + Calculus + Neural Networks) - 2 weeks
2. **Andrew Ng's ML Specialization** (Coursera) - 4 weeks
3. **Fast.ai Practical Deep Learning** - 4 weeks
4. **Andrej Karpathy's Zero to Hero** (including Let's Build GPT) - 3 weeks
5. **Full Stack Deep Learning** (production) - 2 weeks
6. **Chip Huyen's "Designing ML Systems"** (book) - 2 weeks

This sequence takes you from zero to production-capable in ~4 months of focused study.

## Tips for Effective Learning

1. **Code everything** - Don't just watch. Implement from scratch at least once.
2. **Spaced repetition** - Review concepts after 1 day, 3 days, 1 week, 1 month.
3. **Teach others** - Write blog posts or explain to a rubber duck.
4. **Build projects** - Apply each concept to a personal project immediately.
5. **Read papers early** - Start reading papers in Phase 4, even if you don't understand everything.
6. **Join communities** - Kaggle forums, Reddit r/MachineLearning, Discord servers.
7. **Focus on fundamentals** - Frameworks change, math and intuition don't.
