# 12 - Research Skills

## Why Research Skills Matter for Staff Architects

The ML/AI field moves at an unprecedented pace. A technique that was state-of-the-art six months ago
may already be superseded. Staff architects who can efficiently consume, evaluate, and apply research
have a decisive competitive advantage.

## What You'll Learn

| Module | Topic | Key Outcome |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 01 | How to Read ML Papers | Efficiently extract value from academic literature |
| 02 | Key Papers You Must Read | Build foundational knowledge of landmark contributions |
| 03 | Reproducibility & Experiments | Design rigorous, reproducible experiments |
| 04 | Writing ML Papers | Communicate research effectively |

## Why This Matters for Industry

1. **Technology Selection** - Evaluate whether a new paper's claims translate to production value
2. **Architecture Decisions** - Understand theoretical foundations behind system choices
3. **Team Leadership** - Guide teams through literature and establish reading culture
4. **Innovation** - Identify research that can be adapted for business impact
5. **Vendor Evaluation** - Cut through marketing hype with paper-level understanding

## The Research-to-Production Pipeline

```
Paper Published → Read & Evaluate → Prototype → Benchmark → Production Design → Deploy
     ↑                                                              |
     └──────────── Feedback: what works in practice ────────────────┘
```

## How to Use This Section

- Start with Module 01 to build your paper-reading workflow
- Use Module 02 as a curated reading list (work through over weeks/months)
- Apply Module 03 when running experiments or evaluating claims
- Use Module 04 if you publish internal or external research

## Time Investment

- Module 01: 2-3 hours to learn, lifetime habit
- Module 02: 2-4 papers per week, ongoing
- Module 03: Reference as needed during experimentation
- Module 04: When writing papers or technical reports
